Research

LSAMP Research Fellow – Cochlear Implant Path Planning

As a current LSAMP Research Fellow, I have introduced a machine learning component to an ongoing cochlear implant simulation project. I use physical electrode data to train a model that enhances the precision of path planning algorithms for cochlear insertion.

Future work focuses on enhancing device compatibility and further minimizing insertion trauma through anatomical adaptability and machine learning integration.

CVRC Undergraduate Research Assistant – Cochlear Implant Modeling

Before joining LSAMP, I contributed to the same cochlear implant project as a research assistant in the CVRC lab. My focus was on foundational modeling, including MRI segmentation, anatomical 3D modeling, and simulation setup.

I developed anatomical models using ITK-SNAP, refined them in Blender and SolidWorks, and used MATLAB for visualization. I also built a Python script using Trimesh, NumPy, and Matplotlib to process STL files and explore early path planning strategies.
